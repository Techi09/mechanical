01 — Definition

What is mechanical engineering?

A diverse, fundamental discipline that blends physics, mathematics, and material science to design and analyse physical systems. Mechanical engineers design, manufacture, test, and maintain machines — turning an idea on paper into functioning, real-world technology. It's often called the "parent branch" of engineering because its core principles — mechanics, thermodynamics, materials — underpin almost every other engineering field.

SHEET 12
12 — Questions

Frequently asked

Is mechanical engineering still relevant with all the hype around CS/IT?

Yes — every physical product (vehicles, machines, energy systems, medical devices) still needs mechanical engineers to design and build it. The field is also merging with software through robotics, mechatronics, and EV systems, so core-mechanical graduates with some coding skills are in a strong position.

Core company job or IT job after mechanical engineering?

Both are open to you. "Core" roles (automotive, manufacturing, energy, aerospace) use your mechanical training directly. Many mechanical graduates also move into IT, data, or consulting roles — recruiters value the analytical and problem-solving foundation either way.

Government college or private college — which is better value?

Government institutes (IITs, NITs, UAE University) are usually far cheaper and carry strong brand recognition, but seats are limited and competitive. Private institutes (BITS, VIT, AUS) cost more but often offer more flexible curricula, modern campuses, and guaranteed seats if you meet the entrance bar.

Do I need to know CAD software before I join?

No. CAD tools like SolidWorks and AutoCAD are taught from the first year. Some prior exposure helps, but it's not a prerequisite for admission.

Can I change my specialization later (e.g. into robotics or automotive)?

Yes. Most programs keep the first two years broad and let you choose electives and a specialization track from year three onward, so you have time to explore before committing.
